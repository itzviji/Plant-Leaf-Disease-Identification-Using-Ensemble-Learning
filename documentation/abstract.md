# 📄 Abstract

Plant diseases pose a significant threat to agricultural productivity, often leading to reduced crop yield and economic losses for farmers. Early and accurate detection of plant leaf diseases is essential for effective disease management and sustainable farming practices. This project presents an intelligent plant leaf disease identification and classification system that combines **Deep Learning** and **Ensemble Learning** techniques to improve prediction accuracy.

The proposed approach utilizes **ResNet50** as the feature extraction backbone and incorporates the **Mish Activation Function** to enhance feature learning and model convergence. To further improve classification performance and robustness, multiple ensemble learning techniques, including **AdaBoost**, **Bagging**, **Hard Voting**, and **Soft Voting**, are employed. The model is trained and evaluated using the publicly available **PlantVillage Dataset**, containing thousands of healthy and diseased leaf images across multiple crop species.

To provide an accessible and user-friendly solution, a **React Native** application and a web interface were developed, enabling users to upload plant leaf images and receive real-time disease predictions along with preventive recommendations. The proposed system achieved an overall classification accuracy of **98.3%**, demonstrating its effectiveness in identifying plant diseases with high reliability.

This work has been published as an **IEEE conference paper**, highlighting the potential of artificial intelligence and ensemble learning techniques in supporting precision agriculture and assisting farmers in making informed decisions for crop health management.
